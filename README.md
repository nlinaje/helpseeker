# HelpSeeker - Project Overview

## What is HelpSeeker?

HelpSeeker is an iPad app designed to help children with autism (ages 6-12) learn how to ask for help in various real-life situations. Through interactive scenarios featuring a customizable companion character, children practice recognizing when they need help and expressing that need appropriately.

## The Problem We're Solving

Children with autism often face significant challenges in help-seeking behaviors:

- **Difficulty recognizing when help is needed**: They may not identify situations where assistance would be appropriate
- **Struggles with expression**: Even when they recognize they need help, articulating that need can be difficult
- **Social anxiety**: Asking for help involves social interaction, which can cause stress
- **Limited practice opportunities**: Real-world situations happen unpredictably and don't allow for repeated practice
- **Safety concerns**: Inability to communicate needs in emergency or uncomfortable situations

Parents and therapists currently lack structured tools to teach these skills in a safe, repeatable, and engaging way.

## Our Solution

HelpSeeker provides a structured, game-like environment where children can:

1. **Practice in a safe space**: No real-world consequences for mistakes
2. **Repeat scenarios**: Learn through repetition and reinforcement
3. **Use their preferred communication method**: Choice-based (tapping) or speech-based responses
4. **Build confidence**: Gradual progression from easy to challenging situations
5. **Stay engaged**: Character companionship and reward systems maintain motivation

## How It Works

### Core Experience

1. **Character Creation**: The child creates a companion character (animal, robot, fantasy creature, or abstract shape), names it, and customizes its appearance.

2. **Scenario Practice**: The app presents situations where the character needs help:
   - *Physical*: "[Character] sees a book on a high shelf but can't reach it"
   - *Academic*: "[Character] doesn't understand a math problem"
   - *Social*: "[Character] is lost and can't find the classroom"
   - *Emotional*: "[Character] feels overwhelmed by a loud noise"

3. **Interactive Response**: The child helps the character by selecting or saying the appropriate response:
   - **Choice Mode**: Tap the correct phrase from options
   - **Speech Mode**: Say the help request out loud
   - **Adaptive Mode**: Starts with choices, progresses to speech

4. **Immediate Feedback**: 
   - Correct responses trigger celebration animations with the character
   - Incorrect responses provide gentle correction and encouragement
   - Progressive hints guide toward the right answer

5. **Rewards & Progression**:
   - Complete scenarios to unlock educational mini-games
   - Track progress across different situation types
   - Build streaks and earn achievements
   - Unlock character outfits as additional rewards

### Key Features

- **20 Carefully Designed Scenarios**: Covering physical, academic, social, and emotional situations across 5 difficulty levels
- **Speech Recognition**: Fuzzy matching accepts variations of correct phrases
- **Multi-User Support**: Multiple children can have separate profiles on the same device
- **Therapist/Parent Planning Dashboard**: Pre-configure activities, select specific scenarios, plan weekly sessions, control reward game access
- **Privacy-First**: All data stays local on the device; no internet connection required
- **Localization**: German and Spanish languages supported (German priority)
- **100% Free & Open Source**: No payments, no ads, no subscriptions - completely free

## Evidence-Based Approach

HelpSeeker is designed based on established autism intervention principles:

- **Visual Learning**: Concrete scenarios with visual character representations
- **Repetition & Reinforcement**: Structured practice with positive feedback loops
- **Generalization Support**: Diverse scenarios help transfer skills to real life
- **Reducing Anxiety**: Familiar character provides comfort; practice builds confidence
- **Self-Monitoring**: Children see their own progress and achievements
- **Prompt Fading**: Gradual reduction of support (hints) as skills improve

## Therapist & Parent Planning Dashboard

HelpSeeker includes a comprehensive planning interface that allows therapists and parents to pre-configure activities before the child begins their session:

### Session Planning
- **Select Specific Scenarios**: Choose from the scenario library filtered by category, difficulty, or skill targeted
- **Configure Reward Games**: Decide which educational games are available and when they unlock
- **Configure Speech Requirements**: Enable/disable speech in games globally or per game (e.g., "Say answers out loud in math games")
- **Set Session Structure**: Plan the order of activities and estimated duration
- **Weekly Scheduling**: Create recurring session plans (e.g., "Monday: 3 scenarios + letter games")

### Child Mode Lockdown
When the child uses the app, they see ONLY the pre-selected activities:
- No access to unplanned scenarios (reduces overwhelm)
- No unrestricted game access (maintains therapeutic focus)
- Clear, distraction-free interface
- Reduced anxiety through predictability

### Progress Tracking & Collaboration
- Real-time completion updates
- Track progress toward IEP goals
- Share plans between therapist and parent
- Export progress data for therapy sessions
- Trend analysis and pattern identification

### Benefits
- **Structured Therapy**: Pre-planned sessions ensure focus and goal alignment
- **Consistency**: Same activities at home and in therapy sessions
- **Data-Driven**: Adjust plans based on actual progress
- **Reduced Anxiety**: Child knows exactly what to expect

## Educational Reward Games - Unified Platform Library

**Important**: All SpectrumSkills applications share a **single, unified reward games library**. Games unlocked in one app are immediately available in all other apps on the platform.

After completing therapeutic activities in any app (HelpSeeker, EmotionExplorer, DailyLiving, etc.), children unlock access to educational mini-games from the shared platform library. These games adapt to the child's age and developmental level:

### How the Unified System Works

```
Child plays HelpSeeker → Completes scenario → Earns stars → Unlocks "Letter Match"
                                                                    ↓
Child plays EmotionExplorer → "Letter Match" already available → Unlocks "Sound Matching"
                                                                    ↓
Child plays DailyLiving → Both games available → Progress continues seamlessly
```

**Key Benefits**:
- ✅ **No Duplicates**: One game library across all apps
- ✅ **Shared Progress**: Stars and unlocks work everywhere
- ✅ **Therapist Flexibility**: Can use multiple apps without separate reward systems
- ✅ **Child Motivation**: Progress continues regardless of which app they use

### Game Categories (Platform-Wide Library)

#### Literacy & Language
- **Letter Recognition**: Identify letters, trace letter shapes, match uppercase/lowercase
- **Word Building**: Simple spelling games with visual cues
- **Story Sequencing**: Arrange picture cards in logical order

#### Numeracy & Math
- **Number Recognition**: Count objects, identify numerals, number tracing
- **Basic Operations**: Simple addition/subtraction with visual aids
- **Pattern Completion**: Continue number sequences (2, 4, 6, _?)
- **Comparisons**: More/less, bigger/smaller, longer/shorter

#### Cognitive Skills
- **Sound Matching**: Hear an instrument or animal sound, identify the source among 3-4 images
- **Color & Shape Recognition**: Match, sort, and identify colors and geometric shapes
- **Size Matching**: Order objects by size, match same-sized items
- **Logic Patterns**: Identify what comes next in visual sequences (shapes, colors, objects)
- **Memory Games**: Classic matching pairs with adjustable difficulty
- **Memory Match with Speech**: Flip cards and say the name out loud before matching - combines memory, vocabulary, and speech practice with speech recognition

### Speech Integration in All Games (Configurable)

**All reward games integrate speech practice** - children must say answers or names out loud before completing game actions. This transforms play time into speech therapy time!

**Game-Specific Speech Requirements:**
- **Memory Match**: "Apple" (say the card name)
- **Math Games**: "Four" (say the answer to 2+2)
- **Letter Games**: "B" or "Buh" (say the letter)
- **Number Games**: "Twenty-three" (say the number)
- **Sound Games**: "Piano" (say the sound source)
- **Pattern Games**: "Red circle" (say what comes next)
- **Sorting Games**: "Blue" (say the category)

**Therapist Control:**
- ✅ **Enable/Disable**: Turn speech requirement on/off per game or globally
- ✅ **Skip Option**: Allow children to skip speech when struggling
- ✅ **Progressive**: Start lenient, gradually require speech
- ✅ **Non-Verbal Friendly**: All games work without speech when disabled

**How It Works:**
```
Child plays math game → Sees "2 + 2" → Says "Four" 
                              ↓
                    Speech recognition verifies
                              ↓
              ✅ Match → Continue playing
              ❌ No match → Try again or skip
```

#### Spatial Concepts
- **Prepositions**: Learn "in front of," "behind," "below," "above," "next to," "between" by moving objects around a scene
- **Directional Concepts**: Left/right, up/down through interactive puzzles
- **Map Skills**: Simple navigation following directions

#### Problem Solving
- **Sorting & Categorizing**: Group items by attributes (color, type, size)
- **Simple Mazes**: Navigate from start to finish
- **Cause & Effect**: Predict outcomes of simple actions

### Age Adaptation
- **6-7 years**: Focus on letters, numbers 1-10, basic colors/shapes, simple patterns
- **8-9 years**: Addition/subtraction, word recognition, complex patterns, spatial terms
- **10-12 years**: Multiplication concepts, logic puzzles, fractions, advanced problem solving

### Platform Expansion
When new games are added to the SpectrumSkills platform, they automatically become available in **all applications**. There are no app-specific reward games - everything is shared!

These games serve a dual purpose:
1. **Motivation**: Rewards for completing therapeutic practice (any app)
2. **Skill Building**: Additional learning opportunities tailored to each child
3. **Engagement**: Maintains interest through variety and challenge progression
4. **Continuity**: Seamless experience across the entire platform
